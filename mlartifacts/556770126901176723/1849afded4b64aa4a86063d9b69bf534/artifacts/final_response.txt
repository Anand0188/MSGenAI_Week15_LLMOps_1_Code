The Department of Transportation (DOT) enforces the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) with respect to airlines. This means that DOT is responsible for ensuring that airlines comply with COPPA regulations, which prohibit operators of websites from obtaining personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. Additionally, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shares jurisdiction over ticket agents regarding COPPA compliance.

If you need further information about COPPA, you can visit the FTC's COPPA page. If you have specific concerns or questions about a particular airline's practices, feel free to ask!
