A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferrable, meaning it can be passed on to another individual if the original recipient is unable to use it.

3. **Eligibility Conditions**: The voucher is issued when:
   - A consumer is advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease. The ticket must have been purchased before the public health emergency was declared, and the consumer's travel must be scheduled during the emergency to or from the affected area.
   - A consumer is prohibited from travel or is required to quarantine for a substantial portion of the trip by a governmental entity due to a serious communicable disease. The ticket must also have been purchased before the declaration of a public health emergency or before the government prohibition or restriction was imposed.
