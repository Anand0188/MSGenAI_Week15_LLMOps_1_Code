Yes, according to the DOT privacy guidelines, it is considered a 'deceptive' practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed 'deceptive' if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, about a significant aspect of the service or product. This includes situations where the misrepresentation could affect a consumer's decision or conduct regarding their purchase. Therefore, if an airline provides misleading information about its practices, fees, or terms, it could be violating these guidelines.
