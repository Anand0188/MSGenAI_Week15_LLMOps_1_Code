A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The travel voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferable, allowing the recipient to pass it on to someone else if desired.

3. **Conditions of Issuance**:
   - The voucher is issued when a consumer is advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease.
   - The consumer must have purchased the airline ticket before a public health emergency was declared and is scheduled to travel during the emergency to or from the affected area.
   - Alternatively, a voucher may be issued if a consumer is prohibited from travel or required to quarantine for a substantial portion of the trip by a governmental entity in relation to a serious communicable disease, provided the ticket was purchased before the public health emergency or government restriction was imposed.
