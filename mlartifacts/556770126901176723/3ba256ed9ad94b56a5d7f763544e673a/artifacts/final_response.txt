It seems like you're looking for information about travel rules, specifically regarding bookings, cancellations, and the use of credits or vouchers. Here’s a summary of the relevant rules based on the information provided:

1. **Credits and Vouchers**: When using credits or vouchers for travel, airlines may impose certain restrictions. These can include limitations on travel dates, times, or routes. Additionally, a voucher can only be used on one booking and cannot retain any residual value. If a new ticket reduces the value of the voucher, a booking fee may apply.

2. **Disclosure Requirements**: Airlines and ticket agents are required to provide full disclosure about the terms and conditions associated with credits or vouchers. This is to ensure that passengers are aware of any limitations before making a booking.

3. **International and Domestic Carriage**: For international air travel, the rules and limitations relating to liability are governed by the Indian Carriage by Air Act, 1972, and its amendments. For domestic travel, similar regulations apply under the same act and its notifications.

4. **Sales and Refund Policies**: Airlines may conduct audits on ticket sales and refunds to ensure compliance with fare rules. Violations of these rules can result in penalties or the issuance of Agency Debit Memos (ADMs) to recover revenue.
