Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee if a flight is cancelled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but certain conditions must be met. Specifically, the fee retained must be for services that are independent of the ticket's value and not simply for processing payment for the flight. Additionally, the ticket agent must clearly and prominently disclose the existence and amount of this service fee at the time of ticket purchase. 

It's important to note that if the ticket agent fails to disclose the fee properly, it may be considered a deceptive practice. Thus, consumers should be informed about any potential fees that could affect the amount of their refund before they make a purchase. If you have any specific concerns about a fee being retained, it may be helpful to contact the ticket agent directly for clarification.
