Under Air India's GDS misuse policy, "Churning" refers to the process of repeated bookings for the same sector or overlapping sectors for the same passenger. This can occur either through a single Passenger Name Record (PNR) or across multiple PNRs on the same Global Distribution System (GDS) or across various GDSs by an agent.

For bookings created in Economy Class, up to 4 churns are permitted. After that, a penalty of US$10 will be charged for each additional churn done per passenger per segment. For Premium class bookings, the threshold is set at 10 churns. Factors considered to determine churn include the same origin, destination, passenger name, and IATA code.
