Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but under specific conditions. The Department of Transportation allows ticket agents to retain service charges related to issuing the original tickets, as long as these charges are not simply for processing payment for a flight. 

However, it is essential that the nature and amount of these fees are clearly and prominently disclosed to consumers at the time of ticket purchase. If the service fee was not properly disclosed, it could be considered a deceptive practice.
