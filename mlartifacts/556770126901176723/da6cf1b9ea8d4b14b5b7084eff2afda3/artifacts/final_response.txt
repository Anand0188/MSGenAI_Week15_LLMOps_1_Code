Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a 'deceptive' practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed deceptive if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, about something that is material—meaning it could affect the consumer's decisions or conduct regarding a product or service. This includes situations where undisclosed fees or misleading information about refunds could lead consumers to make decisions based on incorrect assumptions.
