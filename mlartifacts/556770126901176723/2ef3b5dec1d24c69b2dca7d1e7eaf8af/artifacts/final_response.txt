Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a 'deceptive' practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is classified as deceptive if it is likely to mislead a consumer, who is acting reasonably under the circumstances, about a material matter—meaning it could affect the consumer’s decision or conduct regarding a product or service. Examples of such misleading practices include failing to disclose important information, such as undisclosed fees or conditions related to ticket refunds.
