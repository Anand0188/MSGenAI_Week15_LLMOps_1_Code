The Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as a change where the passenger is scheduled to arrive at the destination airport six hours or more later than the original scheduled arrival time. This six-hour timeframe is part of a set standard that aims to provide clarity and consistency for both consumers and airlines regarding significant changes to flight itineraries.
