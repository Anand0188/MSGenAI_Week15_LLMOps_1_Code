The Department of Transportation (DOT) enforces the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) with respect to airlines. This means that airlines must comply with COPPA regulations, which prohibit operators of websites from obtaining personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. 

Additionally, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shares jurisdiction over ticket agents with respect to COPPA. If you have further questions or need more specific information about COPPA and its enforcement, I recommend visiting the FTC COPPA Page for additional resources.
