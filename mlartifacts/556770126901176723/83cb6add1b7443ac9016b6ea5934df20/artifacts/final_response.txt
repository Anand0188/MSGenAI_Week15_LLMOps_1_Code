Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a "deceptive" practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is defined as deceptive if it is likely to mislead a consumer who is acting reasonably under the circumstances, particularly in relation to material matters that could affect the consumer's conduct or decisions regarding a product or service. This includes instances where undisclosed fees or misleading information may lead a consumer to incorrect conclusions about their rights or entitlements, such as refunds or compensations.
