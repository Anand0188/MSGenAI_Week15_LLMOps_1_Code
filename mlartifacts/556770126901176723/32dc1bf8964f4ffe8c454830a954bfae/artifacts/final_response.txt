If an airline files for bankruptcy and you are seeking a refund for a ticket purchased with a credit card, you should file a claim with your credit card company as soon as possible. According to the Fair Credit Billing Act, the credit card issuer must receive your notice no later than 60 days after the date you received the first monthly statement that listed the charge for the transportation. 

Make sure to include your account number, a photocopy of the ticket or receipt, and any relevant details about used and unused segments of your travel. Also, specify that the airline is in bankruptcy and that you did not receive the service you paid for.

If you have any further questions or need assistance with the claim process, feel free to ask!
