The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) is responsible for enforcing the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) specifically with respect to airlines. This means that DOT ensures that airlines do not obtain personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. 

Additionally, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shares jurisdiction over ticket agents regarding COPPA compliance. If you would like further information about COPPA or its regulations, you can visit the FTC COPPA Page. If you have specific concerns or need to file a complaint related to COPPA violations by an airline or ticket agent, you may want to contact the appropriate authorities for assistance.
