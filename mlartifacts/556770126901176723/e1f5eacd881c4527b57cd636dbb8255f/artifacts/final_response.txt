Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a 'deceptive' practice for an airline or ticket agent to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed 'deceptive' if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, about something significant that could affect the consumer's decisions or behavior regarding a product or service. 

For example, if an airline fails to disclose important information, such as fees or refund policies, that could lead to a consumer making an uninformed decision, this would likely be classified as a deceptive practice. The DOT has the authority to investigate such complaints and take action against airlines and ticket agents for unfair or deceptive practices.
