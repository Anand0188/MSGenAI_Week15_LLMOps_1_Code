If an airline files for bankruptcy and you used a credit card to purchase your ticket, you should file a claim with your credit card company as soon as possible. According to the Fair Credit Billing Act, the credit card issuer must receive your notice no later than **60 days** after the date you received the first monthly statement that listed the charge for the transportation. 

Make sure to include your account number, a photocopy of the ticket or receipt, identify any used and unused segments if applicable, and state that the airline is in bankruptcy and you did not receive the service that you charged to your card. 

If you have any further questions or need additional guidance, feel free to ask!
