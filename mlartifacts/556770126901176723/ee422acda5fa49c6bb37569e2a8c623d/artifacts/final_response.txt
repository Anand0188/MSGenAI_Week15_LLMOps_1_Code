The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as a change where the passenger is scheduled to arrive at the destination airport six hours or more later than the original scheduled arrival time. This is part of the proposed regulations aimed at providing clarity and consistency in how significant flight schedule changes are defined and handled.
