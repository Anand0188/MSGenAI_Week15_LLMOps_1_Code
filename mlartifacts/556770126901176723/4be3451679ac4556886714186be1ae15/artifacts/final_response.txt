Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but there are specific conditions that must be met. The ticket agent may retain a service charge related to issuing the original ticket as long as that charge is not simply for processing payment for the flight. This service charge represents the cost of service already provided by the ticket agent.

However, it is crucial that the ticket agent clearly and prominently discloses the existence and amount of this service fee at the time of ticket purchase. If the fee is not disclosed, it may be considered a deceptive practice.

In summary, while ticket agents can retain certain service fees when processing refunds for canceled flights, they must adhere to the requirements of clear disclosure to consumers.
