The Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as any change that exceeds six hours from the originally scheduled arrival time. This is part of a proposed approach which sets a specific timeline for significant departure and arrival time changes. Additionally, a significant change can also involve other factors, such as an increase in layover time by more than one hour or an increase in the number of connections in a flight itinerary.
