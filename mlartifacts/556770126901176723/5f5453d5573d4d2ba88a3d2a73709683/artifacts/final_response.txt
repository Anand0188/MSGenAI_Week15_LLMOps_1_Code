Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a "deceptive" practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed "deceptive" if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, about a material matter. A material matter is one that is likely to affect the consumer's conduct or decision regarding a product or service. 

For instance, if an airline fails to disclose certain fees or conditions that may influence a consumer's decision, this could be viewed as a deceptive practice. Therefore, airlines must provide clear and accurate information to their customers to avoid misleading them.
