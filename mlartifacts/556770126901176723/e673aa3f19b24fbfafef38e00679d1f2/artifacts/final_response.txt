For international flights, there are several important baggage rules to keep in mind:

1. **Free Baggage Allowance**: Passengers are allowed to carry a certain amount of baggage free of charge, as specified in the Carrier's Regulations.

2. **Excess Baggage**: If you need to carry more baggage than the free allowance, you will be charged at the rates prescribed in the Carrier's Regulations.

3. **Prohibited Items**: Certain items cannot be included in your checked baggage, such as:
   - Fragile or perishable items
   - Money, jewelry, precious metals, silverware, negotiable papers, and other valuables
   - Business documents, passports, and identification documents
   - Firearms and ammunition (unless for hunting and sporting purposes, and compliant with regulations)

4. **Mishandled Baggage**: If your baggage is delayed, the timeframe for the carrier to return it varies:
   - For international itineraries with a non-stop flight of 12 hours or less, the carrier must make every reasonable effort to return mishandled baggage within 15 hours.
   - For international itineraries with a non-stop flight of more than 12 hours, the carrier has up to 30 hours to return the baggage to avoid refunding baggage fees.

5. **Identification**: It's important to label your checked baggage with your name or other personal identification before handing it over to the carrier.

6. **Carriage of Baggage**: Checked baggage is usually carried on the same aircraft as the passenger. However, if this is not possible, it will be placed on the next available flight.

If you have any specific questions or need more detailed information regarding your flight, feel free to ask!
