Yes, according to the Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is considered a 'deceptive' practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed deceptive if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, about a significant issue that could affect the consumer's decisions regarding a product or service. This includes situations where undisclosed fees or misleading information about refunds could lead a consumer to believe something that is not accurate, potentially resulting in financial loss or confusion.
