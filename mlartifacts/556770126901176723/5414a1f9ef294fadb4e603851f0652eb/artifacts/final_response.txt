The Department of Transportation (DOT) enforces the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) with respect to airlines. This means that airlines must comply with COPPA's regulations, which prohibit them from obtaining personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. In addition to the DOT, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shares jurisdiction over ticket agents concerning COPPA compliance.
