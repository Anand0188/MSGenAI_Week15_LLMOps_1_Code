The Department of Transportation (DOT) enforces the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) specifically with respect to airlines. This means that airlines must comply with COPPA regulations, which prohibit them from obtaining personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. 

Additionally, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) shares jurisdiction over ticket agents regarding COPPA compliance. If you have further questions or need more detailed information about COPPA and its implications, you may want to visit the FTC COPPA Page for comprehensive guidance.
