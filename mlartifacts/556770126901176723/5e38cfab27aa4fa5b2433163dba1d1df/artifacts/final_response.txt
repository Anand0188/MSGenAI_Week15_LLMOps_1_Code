Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee even if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund. According to the regulations, ticket agents are allowed to keep the service fee charged for issuing the original ticket as long as the fee is on a per-passenger basis and was clearly disclosed to consumers at the time of purchase. This service charge is considered separate from the ticket's value and reflects the cost of services already provided by the ticket agent.

However, it's important to note that if the airline cancels or significantly changes a flight, the carrier cannot retain a processing fee for issuing the required refund. In such cases, consumers are entitled to a full refund of the ticket price, but the service fee may still be retained by the ticket agent if it meets the specified conditions.
