Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee if a flight is cancelled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but there are specific conditions that must be met. The ticket agent may retain a service charge related to the issuance of the original ticket, provided that this charge is not simply for processing payment for the flight. 

The key points to consider are:

1. **Disclosure**: The existence and amount of the service fee must be clearly and prominently disclosed to consumers at the time of ticket purchase.

2. **Per-Passenger Basis**: The fee must be assessed on a per-passenger basis.

3. **Nature of Service**: The service charge should represent a cost of service already provided by the ticket agent, independent of whether the passenger ultimately traveled.
