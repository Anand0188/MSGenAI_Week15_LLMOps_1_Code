A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferable, allowing the recipient to pass it on to another person if desired.

3. **Eligibility Criteria**: The voucher can be issued under specific conditions, including:
   - The passenger is advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease.
   - The passenger purchased the airline ticket before a public health emergency was declared and is scheduled to travel during that public health emergency to or from the affected area.
   - The passenger is prohibited from traveling or required to quarantine for a substantial portion of the trip by a governmental entity in relation to a serious communicable disease, and the ticket was purchased before the declaration of a public health emergency or government prohibition/restriction.
