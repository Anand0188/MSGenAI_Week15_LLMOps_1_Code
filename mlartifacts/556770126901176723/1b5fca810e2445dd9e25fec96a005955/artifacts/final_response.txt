A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferrable, allowing the passenger to transfer it to another individual if desired.

3. **Eligibility Conditions**: The voucher can be issued under specific conditions:
   - The passenger must be advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease.
   - The passenger must have purchased the airline ticket before the public health emergency was declared or before any governmental prohibition or restriction on travel was imposed.
   - Alternatively, the passenger must be prohibited from travel or required to quarantine for a substantial portion of their trip by a governmental entity in relation to a serious communicable disease, again having purchased the ticket prior to the declaration or imposition of restrictions.
