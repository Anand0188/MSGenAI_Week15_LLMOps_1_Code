Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee even if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund. However, this is only applicable if the service charge is not merely for processing payment for the flight and if the fee represents the cost of service that has already been provided by the ticket agent. 

It's important that the nature and amount of these fees are clearly and prominently disclosed to consumers at the time of ticket purchase. If such fees are not disclosed, it may be considered a deceptive practice, which could lead to further action by the Department of Transportation.
