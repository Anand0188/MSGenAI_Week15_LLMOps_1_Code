The Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as a change where the passenger is scheduled to arrive at the destination airport six hours or more later than the original scheduled arrival time. This standard is part of a broader regulation that also applies to domestic flights, where a significant change is defined as an arrival time change of three hours or more.
