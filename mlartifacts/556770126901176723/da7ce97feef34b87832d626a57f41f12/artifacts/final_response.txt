A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferrable, allowing the passenger to transfer it to another individual if desired.

3. **Conditions for Issuance**: The voucher is issued under specific conditions:
   - The passenger was advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease.
   - The passenger purchased the airline ticket before a public health emergency was declared and is scheduled to travel during the public health emergency to or from the affected area.
   - Alternatively, if the passenger is prohibited from travel or required to quarantine for a substantial portion of the trip by a governmental entity due to a serious communicable disease, they must have purchased the ticket before the public health emergency declaration or before any government travel prohibitions were imposed.
