A travel voucher issued to a passenger who cannot fly due to a serious communicable disease must have the following mandatory characteristics:

1. **Validity Period**: The voucher must be valid for at least five years from the date of issuance.

2. **Transferability**: The voucher should be transferable, allowing the consumer to pass it on to another individual if desired.

3. **Issuance Conditions**: The travel voucher can be issued under specific conditions, which include:
   - The consumer has been advised by a licensed treating medical professional not to travel during a public health emergency to protect themselves from a serious communicable disease.
   - The consumer purchased the airline ticket before a public health emergency was declared and is scheduled to travel during that emergency to or from the affected area.
   - Alternatively, the voucher can be issued if the consumer is prohibited from travel or is required to quarantine for a substantial portion of the trip by a governmental entity due to a serious communicable disease, provided the ticket was purchased before the relevant public health emergency or governmental restriction was imposed.
