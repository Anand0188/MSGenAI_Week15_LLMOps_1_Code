The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) enforces the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) with respect to airlines. This means that airlines must comply with COPPA regulations, which prohibit them from obtaining personal information from children under the age of 13 without first obtaining parental consent. 

Additionally, DOT shares jurisdiction with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) regarding ticket agents in relation to COPPA compliance. If you have further questions about COPPA or need specific details, you can visit the FTC's COPPA page for more information.
