The Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as a change where the passenger is scheduled to arrive at the destination airport six hours or more later than the original scheduled arrival time. This definition is part of a broader regulation that establishes specific timeframes for what constitutes a significant departure or arrival change for both domestic and international flights.
