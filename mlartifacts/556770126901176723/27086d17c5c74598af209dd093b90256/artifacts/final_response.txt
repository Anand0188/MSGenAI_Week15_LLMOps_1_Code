Yes, a ticket agent can retain a service fee if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but there are specific conditions that must be met. The ticket agent may retain a fee charged when issuing the original ticket as long as this fee is for services beyond just processing the payment for the flight. 

Importantly, the fee must be clearly and prominently disclosed to consumers at the time of ticket purchase, including its amount and whether it is non-refundable. If these conditions are met, the ticket agent is allowed to retain the service fee even if the passenger did not travel due to the cancellation.
