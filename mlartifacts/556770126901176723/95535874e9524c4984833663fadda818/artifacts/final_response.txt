The Department of Transportation (DOT) defines a "significant change" for an international flight's arrival time as a time change of more than six hours. This means that if the projected arrival time of the flight is altered by more than six hours from the original scheduled arrival time, it would qualify as a significant change. Additionally, any increase in layover time by more than one hour or an increase in the number of connections in the flight itinerary can also be considered significant changes.
