Yes, a ticket agent may retain a service fee if a flight is canceled and the passenger is entitled to a refund, but certain conditions must be met. The ticket agent can retain this fee only if it is related to the service of issuing the original ticket (not just for processing payment) and if the fee is clearly and prominently disclosed to consumers at the time of ticket purchase. 

It's important to note that the fee must be assessed on a per-passenger basis. If the ticket agent fails to disclose the existence and amount of the fee, it may be considered a deceptive practice, which could lead to further action by the Department of Transportation.
