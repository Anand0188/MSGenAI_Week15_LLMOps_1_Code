For international flights, here are the key baggage rules to keep in mind:

1. **Delay Refunds**: If your checked baggage is delivered to you with a delay, you are entitled to a refund of any baggage fees paid if the delay exceeds:
   - 15 hours for international flights with a duration of 12 hours or less.
   - 30 hours for international flights with a duration of over 12 hours.

2. **Mishandling Rates**: It's important to note that mishandled baggage incidents are more common on international flights compared to domestic flights, with a rate of 19.3 incidents per thousand passengers on international routes.

3. **Checked Baggage**: 
   - When you check baggage, the airline will issue a Baggage Identification Tag for each piece.
   - Baggage must have some form of personal identification affixed to it prior to acceptance by the airline.
   - Checked baggage will generally be carried on the same aircraft as the passenger unless it's impractical, in which case it will be sent on the next available flight.

4. **Free Baggage Allowance**: Each airline has its own regulations regarding the free baggage allowance, so it's best to check with your specific airline for details on how much baggage you can carry without additional charges.

5. **Excess Baggage**: If your baggage exceeds the free allowance, you will incur additional charges as specified by the airline's regulations.

6. **Prohibited Items**: Certain items are not allowed as checked baggage, including fragile or perishable items, firearms (except for hunting purposes under specific conditions), and valuables such as money, jewelry, and important documents.

For the most accurate and specific information, I recommend checking with your airline's official website or contacting their customer service directly, as baggage policies can vary between carriers. If you have any more questions or need further assistance, feel free to ask!
