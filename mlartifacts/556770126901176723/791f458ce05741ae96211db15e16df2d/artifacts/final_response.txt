Yes, according to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) privacy guidelines, it is indeed considered a "deceptive" practice for an airline to mislead a consumer regarding a material matter. A practice is deemed deceptive if it is likely to mislead a consumer, acting reasonably under the circumstances, regarding an important issue. This includes situations where the misleading information could affect the consumer's decision or behavior concerning a product or service. Therefore, if an airline fails to provide clear and truthful information that could influence a consumer's choices, it could be in violation of DOT guidelines on deceptive practices.
